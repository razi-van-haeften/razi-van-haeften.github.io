Why was the Dardanelles Strait an important strategic target

World War 1 featured 2 main opposing alliances, the Allies and the Central Powers.  The Allies were initially composed of France, Russia, and the British Empire. Italy and the United States joined in later years.  The Central Powers consisted of Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria and the Ottoman Empire.  In early 1915, Grand Duke Nicholas, Commander of the Russian armies requested aid from the British Empire in the hope that they would help relieve them of Turkish resistence in Armenia.  The German Imperial Army had previously won a major victory at the Battle of Tannenberg in East Prussia, forcing the Russians downwards into the Caucasus where they were vulnerable to Turkish forces.  Controlling the Dardenelles Strait would enable reliable communications with Russia as well as the re-opening of shipping routes.
